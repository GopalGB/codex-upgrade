# prod-delegation

Delegation isn't dumping tasks-it's transferring ownership of an outcome with the right amount of authority. Use the **7 levels of delegation** (Appelo's Delegation Poker) to set authority explicitly: 1 Tell, 2 Sell, 3 Consult, 4 Agree, 5 Advise, 6 Inquire, 7 Delegate fully. State the level up front so expectations match-most conflict comes from the delegator thinking level 7 while the delegatee assumed level 2. Hand off with an **outcome brief**: the goal and why it matters (context), the definition of done, constraints/budget, the decision rights, and the check-in cadence-then resist re-specifying *how*. Clarify ownership with **RACI** (Responsible/Accountable/Consulted/Informed); there can be only one Accountable. Watch for **reverse delegation** ('the monkey on the back', Oncken): when a report says 'I'm stuck', do NOT take the task back-ask 'what do you recommend?' and keep the next move theirs. The two failure modes: micromanaging (delegating the task but not the authority, so you're still the bottleneck) and abdicating (handing off with no context/check-ins, then being surprised). Delegate outcomes and authority together, inspect at checkpoints, not continuously.

**Tools:** levels of delegation, RACI, delegation poker, monkey-on-the-back, outcome briefs
