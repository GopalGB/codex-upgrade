# retail-omnichannel-bopis

Omnichannel makes one inventory pool serve every channel. **BOPIS** (buy online, pick up in store) and **ship-from-store** turn stores into mini-DCs. The foundation is **unified/network-available-to-promise (ATP)** inventory — a single accurate on-hand per location that web, app, and POS all read, or you'll oversell (promise stock that just sold in-store).

The expert move: a **distributed order management (DOM)** system routes each order to the optimal node by a cost function — proximity (shipping cost/speed), on-hand depth, **markdown risk** (fulfill from a store overstocked on that SKU to avoid its markdown), and labor capacity. Reserve **safety buffers per store** so in-store shoppers and BOPIS don't fight over the last unit; expose 'available for pickup' only above buffer.

KPIs: BOPIS attach (% who buy more in-store at pickup — the halo), pick accuracy, on-time-pickup-ready, and ship-from-store split shipments. **Endless aisle** lets a store sell SKUs it doesn't stock by drawing from the network.

Pitfalls: inventory inaccuracy (BOPIS cancellations destroy trust — gate on cycle-count accuracy first), no store labor model for picking (orders pile up), and counting BOPIS as e-comm revenue while store labor bears the cost (mis-attribution skews channel P&L). Measure true incrementality, not channel-shift.

**Tools:** distributed order management, ATP inventory, ship-from-store routing, click-and-collect
