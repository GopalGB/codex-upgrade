# retail-private-label

Private label (own brand) is a margin and differentiation engine — PL typically carries **5-15+ points higher gross margin** than national brands because there's no brand-marketing markup in the chain, and it's exclusive (can't be price-checked elsewhere).

The expert move: set the **price gap vs the national-brand benchmark** deliberately — usually **15-30% below** the leading NB on a comparable-quality value/standard PL (enough to switch the shopper, not so deep it signals 'cheap/inferior'). Tier the PL line **good-better-best**: a **value/opening-price** tier (price fighter vs discounters), a **standard 'national-brand-equivalent'** tier (the volume core), and a **premium/specialty** tier (margin + halo, often quality-led). Track **PL penetration** (PL units or $ / category total) as the growth KPI.

Manage the trade-offs: PL needs reliable sourcing/QA (you own the quality risk and recalls), demand forecasting (no NB vendor to share risk or give markdown money), and shelf placement (block PL beside the NB it targets so the price/value comparison is obvious at the moment of choice).

Pitfalls: pricing PL too close to the NB (no switching incentive, cannibalizes nothing useful) or too far below (margin left on table + 'cheap' perception), over-penetrating so you lose the NBs that build category traffic and credibility, and skimping on quality (a bad PL experience poisons trust in the whole store brand). Balance PL penetration against category role — destination categories still need strong NBs.

**Tools:** private-label margin uplift, national-brand price gap, good-better-best PL tiering, penetration %
