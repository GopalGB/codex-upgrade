# career-salary-negotiation

Almost every tech offer has negotiation room — recruiters expect it, and the first number is rarely the ceiling. **Never accept (or give a number) on the spot.** Defer salary expectations until you have an offer ('I'd like to learn more about the role first; I'm sure we can find a fair number'). Negotiate **total comp**, not just base: base + equity (vesting + refreshers) + target bonus + sign-on. Sign-on bonus and equity often flex more than base.

Do the homework: benchmark with levels.fyi/Glassdoor by company, level, and location; know the band. **Anchor first if forced**, slightly above your target. The strongest lever is a **competing offer** (or active interviews) — leverage is the entire game. Negotiate by email when possible (time to think, written record). Be collaborative, not adversarial: 'I'm excited to join — to get there, can we get base to X / sign-on to Y?' Get the final offer in writing before resigning.

**What top candidates do differently:** they run interviews in parallel so offers overlap (real competing leverage), negotiate every component (not just base), and stay genuinely warm — recruiters fight harder for candidates they like.

**Common mistake:** disclosing current/expected salary too early (anchors you low), accepting the first offer out of relief or fear of rescission (extremely rare), bluffing a competing offer you can't substantiate, and negotiating aggressively/rudely. One email asking confidently can be worth tens of thousands.

**Tools:** total-comp framing, levels.fyi benchmarks, anchoring high, competing-offer leverage, never-accept-on-the-spot
