# research-mixed-methods

Pick a named design by your purpose and the timing/priority of strands. **Convergent parallel**: collect qual + quant concurrently, analyze separately, then merge to corroborate or explain divergence. **Explanatory sequential** (QUANT -> qual): run numbers first, then interviews to explain surprising results. **Exploratory sequential** (QUAL -> quant): explore qualitatively to build an instrument/hypotheses, then test quantitatively. Capitalize timing as the priority strand (QUANT+qual = quant-dominant).

The defining skill is **integration**, not just doing both methods. Use a **joint display** — a table/figure putting quantitative results beside qualitative themes for the same construct — to produce **meta-inferences** that neither strand alone yields. Plan integration at design, methods (e.g., sampling the qual subset from quant respondents), and interpretation levels.

Align sampling: large probability sample for quant, purposive subsample for qual, usually nested.

Pitfall: "parallel studies stapled together" — two findings sections with no integration. If you can't point to a joint display or meta-inference, it isn't mixed methods. Second pitfall: ignoring divergence between strands; contradictions are findings (they reveal boundary conditions), not errors to hide.

**Tools:** Creswell typologies, joint display, triangulation, sequential/concurrent design, meta-inference, sampling alignment
