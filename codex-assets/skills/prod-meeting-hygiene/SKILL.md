# prod-meeting-hygiene

Default-to-async; a meeting must justify why it can't be a document. Gate every meeting on three things or cancel it: a stated **purpose/decision to make**, a written **agenda** sent ahead, and a named **DRI/owner** plus the minimum required attendees (if someone's only there 'to be informed', send notes instead). Run it timeboxed, end with explicit **decisions, owners, and due dates** logged in a persistent decision log. Prefer **async** for status updates (written), broad input (commented doc/RFC), and anything not needing real-time back-and-forth; reserve sync for debate, brainstorming, relationship-building, and sensitive conversations. Protect **maker time** with org-wide no-meeting blocks/days so engineers and writers get uninterrupted spans-fragmented calendars destroy deep work even if total meeting hours are low. Decision frameworks (DACI: Driver/Approver/Contributors/Informed) prevent the diffuse-ownership failure where a meeting ends with no one accountable. The dominant failure mode is the recurring meeting that outlives its purpose-audit recurring meetings quarterly and kill any without a current decision to make.

**Tools:** meeting agenda, DACI/RACI, async-first, RFC docs, decision log, no-meeting days
