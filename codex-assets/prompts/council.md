# /council — 5-advisor pressure-test (no yes-men)

Decision to pressure-test: $ARGUMENTS

Play 5 advisors IN FULL, then peer review, then a chairman synthesis. At least one
advisor MUST dissent substantively. Unanimity is suspect — if all 5 agree, re-frame
harder and re-run.

**ADVISOR 1 — CONTRARIAN** (150-300w): argue the strongest case for the OPPOSITE.
End: "The fatal flaw is: ___"
**ADVISOR 2 — FIRST PRINCIPLES** (150-300w): strip the framing; restate from basics.
End: "The first-principles flaw is: ___"
**ADVISOR 3 — EXPANSIONIST** (150-300w): the biggest upside this leaves on the table.
End: "The expansionist flaw is: ___"
**ADVISOR 4 — OUTSIDER** (150-300w): zero context, cold read — engage or walk away?
End: "The outsider flaw is: ___"
**ADVISOR 5 — EXECUTOR** (150-300w): day 1-14 sequence; where's the first failure?
End: "The executor flaw is: ___"

**PEER REVIEW** (100-200w): which flaw is REAL vs noise; one blind spot all 5 missed.
**CHAIRMAN** (200-300w): verdict SHIP / SHIP-WITH-CAVEATS / KILL / RE-FRAME; up to 3
caveats; the ONE concrete first step in the next 60 minutes.

Required context to use (ask for any that's missing): the decision, current state +
constraints, what goes wrong if it's the wrong call.
