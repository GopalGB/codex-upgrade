# retail-shrink-loss-prevention

**Shrink % = (book inventory value - physical count value) / net sales** at retail. Industry shrink runs roughly 1.5-2% of sales; every point of shrink is nearly pure profit lost. Sources split roughly into **external theft, internal/employee theft, administrative/paperwork error, and vendor fraud/damage** — you cannot fix shrink without knowing the mix.

The expert move: don't treat shrink as one number. Reconcile **book-to-physical variance** by category and store, then attribute: high-value/concealable SKUs (electronics, cosmetics, razors) point to theft; systemic SKU-level negatives point to **scan/receiving/pricing errors** (often the biggest, most fixable chunk). Tighten receiving (count-in vs PO), POS exception reporting (voids, no-sales, refunds without item), and cycle-count high-shrink SKUs weekly rather than waiting for annual physical.

Pitfalls: blaming theft for what is really process error (wrong fix, no improvement), annual-only counts (you find the hole months after it opened), and EAS/locking high-shrink items without measuring sales suppression (over-securing kills sales of the very items that drive trips). Track shrink trend by store and tie LP staffing to it.

**Tools:** shrink % formula, book-to-physical variance, source-of-shrink split, cycle counts
