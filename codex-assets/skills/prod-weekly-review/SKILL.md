# prod-weekly-review

The weekly review is the keystone habit that keeps any productivity system trustworthy; without it lists go stale and you stop believing them. Block a fixed 30-90 min recurring slot (Friday afternoon or Sunday evening). Run a checklist in three passes. **Get Clear**: empty every inbox (email, notes, physical, browser tabs, camera roll) to zero; process loose items into next actions. **Get Current**: review the calendar 2 weeks back (capture follow-ups) and 4-6 weeks forward (prep upcoming commitments); review every active project for a defined next action; review Waiting-For and Someday/Maybe lists. **Get Creative**: add new ideas/projects, and do a brief retrospective-what worked, what to change. End by selecting next week's 3-5 priorities and time-blocking them. The failure mode is skipping it when busy-which is exactly when it matters most, because a busy week is what fills inboxes and breaks the system. Treat it as non-negotiable infrastructure; a 45-minute review recovers hours of scattered re-deciding across the week.

**Tools:** weekly review, GTD review checklist, inbox-zero pass, week planning
