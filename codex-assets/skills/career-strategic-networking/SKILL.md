# career-strategic-networking

~50%+ of jobs flow through networks, and weak ties (acquaintances, not close friends) surface the most novel opportunities (Granovetter). Build the network *before* you need it. **Give-first:** offer help, share useful resources, amplify others' work — reciprocity compounds. Tactics: request **informational interviews** (15 min, ask about their path/team, never ask for a job — referrals follow naturally), engage in communities (niche Discords/Slacks, local meetups, conferences), build a public presence (OSS contributions, technical blogging, conference talks — inbound network), and re-activate dormant ties with a specific, low-friction message.

For warm intros: ask a mutual connection for a **double-opt-in intro** and supply a forwardable blurb. Track relationships lightly; follow up with value, not just asks.

**What top candidates do differently:** they network continuously (not only while job-hunting), focus on giving before taking, and nurture weak ties broadly rather than over-investing in a few close ones. A public artifact (blog, OSS, talk) makes the network find *them*.

**Common mistake:** only reaching out when you need something (transactional, low yield), asking strangers directly for a job, neglecting the network between searches so it's cold when needed, and treating it as numbers-collecting rather than relationship-building. The give-first account must have deposits before withdrawals.

**Tools:** give-first reciprocity, informational interviews, OSS/community presence, weak-ties theory, intro requests
