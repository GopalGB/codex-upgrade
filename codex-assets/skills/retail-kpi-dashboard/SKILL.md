# retail-kpi-dashboard

A retail dashboard is built on a **metric tree** that decomposes sales into actionable drivers: **Sales = Traffic x Conversion x ATV**, and **ATV (avg transaction value) = UPT x AUR**. So **Sales = Traffic x Conversion x UPT x AUR**. Move any one lever and you know exactly why sales changed.

Definitions: **Conversion = transactions / traffic** (door counter or sessions); **UPT = units / transaction** (basket size, driven by attach/cross-sell); **AUR = sales $ / units** (average unit retail, driven by price/mix/markdown); **ATV = sales / transactions**. The expert move: diagnose by walking the tree — sales down but traffic flat means a **conversion or basket** problem (staffing, stockouts, layout), not a marketing/traffic problem; AUR down with units up means **markdown/mix** erosion. This points the fix at the right lever instead of guessing.

Layer in margin (gross margin %, GMROI), inventory (sell-through, WOS, turn), and comp sales. The expert move on layout: lead with the tree, then exceptions (red/green vs plan and vs LY), then drill-downs by store/category.

Pitfalls: vanity totals with no decomposition (you see *what* changed, never *why*), inconsistent traffic source (door counter vs POS-only conversion is meaningless), mixing comp and non-comp, and AUR vs ASP confusion (AUR per unit vs avg selling price per style). Always show vs plan AND vs last year, on the fiscal calendar.

**Tools:** conversion rate, AUR, UPT, ATV, traffic, sales = traffic x conversion x ATV decomposition
