# retail-pricing-strategy

Markup vs margin is the first trap: **margin% = (price - cost)/price**, **markup% on cost = (price - cost)/cost**. Keystone = 2x cost = 50% margin / 100% markup. Compute **price = cost / (1 - target margin%)**, not cost x (1+margin).

Distinguish **IMU** (initial markup, the ticket) from **MMU** (maintained markup after markdowns/shrink) — you must plan IMU high enough that MMU survives reductions: IMU = (expenses + profit + reductions) / (net sales + reductions).

The expert move: use **price elasticity** (% change qty / % change price) to find the revenue-max point. Elastic items (>1, fashion, discretionary) lose revenue when raised; inelastic (<1, staples, destination) can take increases. Test in A/B store groups. Anchor with **psychological endings** — $9.99 vs $10 (left-digit effect), $X.00 for premium signaling, $X.97/.98 for clearance flags.

Pitfalls: cost-plus blindness (ignores willingness-to-pay), uniform % increases across elasticity bands, and price-gap violations vs key competitors on KVIs (known-value items shoppers price-check). Hold KVIs sharp, take margin on the long tail.

**Tools:** keystone markup, IMU/maintained markup, price elasticity of demand, price-ending psychology
