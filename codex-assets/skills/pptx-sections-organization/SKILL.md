# pptx-sections-organization

Sections are folders for slides — essential past ~15 slides. Right-click between thumbnails > Add Section, then Rename (e.g. 'Problem', 'Solution', 'Ask'). Collapse sections to navigate/reorder large decks fast; drag a whole section to reorder a chapter at once. In Slide Sorter view you see all sections as groups — the fastest way to audit deck flow and spot a bloated chapter. Section Zoom (Insert > Zoom > Section Zoom) creates a clickable thumbnail that jumps to a section and returns — turns a linear deck into a navigable, agenda-driven presentation (great for Q&A jumping). You can also apply a different transition per section if needed. Expert use: build the section skeleton FIRST (empty sections named after your narrative beats), then fill — it enforces structure and prevents the 60-slide soup. Pitfall: sections are presentation metadata; exporting to PDF flattens them (but PDF bookmarks can be generated). When merging decks, sections from the source come along — clean up duplicate/empty sections before delivery via right-click > Remove Section (keeps slides).

**Tools:** Home > Section / right-click thumbnail > Add Section; Slide Sorter view; Insert > Section Zoom; Rename Section
