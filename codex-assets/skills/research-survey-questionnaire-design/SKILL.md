# research-survey-questionnaire-design

Map every item to a construct before writing it — no orphan questions. Write one idea per item: kill **double-barreled** items ("Was the staff friendly and efficient?" — split them). Avoid leading wording, loaded terms, and negations ("I do not dislike..."). Match the response scale to the construct: 5- or 7-point **Likert** for agreement, **semantic differential** for bipolar attributes, frequency anchors with concrete labels ("daily / weekly") not vague ones ("often").

Use a balanced scale (equal positive/negative anchors) and decide deliberately on a midpoint and a non-applicable option. Randomize or counterbalance item order to defeat order effects; put sensitive/demographic items last. Reverse-code some items to catch straight-lining, but watch the acquiescence trap.

The expert move: **cognitive interviewing** (think-aloud with 5-8 target respondents) before fielding — it surfaces misread items no pilot statistic catches. Then pilot (n~30+) and check **Cronbach's alpha** (>= .70) per multi-item scale, plus exploratory factor analysis to confirm items load on intended factors.

Pitfall: measuring attitude with a single item then treating it as reliable. Multi-item scales for any latent construct.

**Tools:** Likert scales, Qualtrics/Google Forms, cognitive interviewing, Cronbach's alpha, factor analysis
