# prod-saying-no-boundaries

Every yes is a no to everything else you'd do with that time-overcommitment is the silent killer of focus. Use Derek Sivers' **Hell Yeah or No** filter for discretionary opportunities: if it isn't a clear 'hell yeah', it's a no; this keeps your calendar free for the rare great things. Decline gracefully with William Ury's **Positive No** structure (Yes-No-Yes): affirm the underlying value or relationship ('I care about this team'), state the clear boundary ('I can't take this on this quarter'), then offer an alternative or path ('here's who could, or I can in Q3'). Make the **opportunity cost** explicit when asked to add work: 'Yes, and which of my current commitments should I drop to make room?'-this forces the requester to own the tradeoff. Pre-commit **boundaries** so you decide once, not every time: protected deep-work blocks, no-meeting days, response-time norms. The dominant failure modes: explaining/over-justifying (invites negotiation-keep the no short and warm), and the 'maybe later' that's really a yes you'll resent. A timely honest no preserves trust better than a yes you can't deliver.

**Tools:** positive no, Hell Yeah or No, opportunity cost, boundary scripts, calendar defense
