# retail-seasonality-454-calendar

Retail plans on the **NRF 4-5-4 calendar**, not Gregorian months. Each quarter = months of 4, 5, and 4 weeks (13 weeks), so every period ends on the same weekday (Saturday) and contains the same number of weekends — critical because weekends drive traffic. This makes **week-over-week and year-over-year comparisons apples-to-apples**.

The expert move: align to **comparable weeks** (this year's Week 27 vs last year's Week 27), and watch the **53rd week** — every 5-6 years a leap week is added so the calendar doesn't drift off Saturday-end; that year has a 'phantom' extra week that inflates annual totals (back it out for true comp). Shift holidays to their **fiscal week** (Easter and the position of major holidays move between fiscal weeks year to year — a calendar shift, not real growth/decline).

Use **comp/like-for-like sales** (same stores open both periods) to strip out store-count growth, and **seasonal indices** to spread an annual plan across periods.

Pitfalls: comparing fiscal to Gregorian months (instant noise), missing a holiday's week-shift (you'll mis-read a 'decline' that's just timing), forgetting the 53-week year, and comping new+closed stores into 'comp' sales (inflates the metric). Always state which calendar and whether sales are comp.

**Tools:** 4-5-4 calendar, fiscal week/period, 53-week year, comp/like-for-like, seasonal index
