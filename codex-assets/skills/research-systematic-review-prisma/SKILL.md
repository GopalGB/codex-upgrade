# research-systematic-review-prisma

Register a protocol in PROSPERO BEFORE screening — post-hoc reviews are not systematic. Specify the PICO/PECO (Population, Intervention/Exposure, Comparator, Outcome), databases, date range, and the exact Boolean strings per database (they differ: MeSH terms for PubMed, Emtree for Embase).

Report against the **PRISMA 2020** 27-item checklist and draw the four-box flow diagram: Identification (records found, duplicates removed) -> Screening (titles/abstracts excluded) -> Eligibility (full texts assessed, with exclusion counts AND reasons) -> Included. Numbers must reconcile down the diagram.

Dual independent screening with a documented kappa for inter-rater agreement; resolve conflicts by a third reviewer. Use Covidence or Rayyan to manage it. Appraise each included study with the right tool: **RoB 2** for RCTs, **ROBINS-I** for non-randomized studies; rate the whole review with AMSTAR 2.

Pitfall: conflating systematic review with meta-analysis — a systematic review may be narrative if studies are too heterogeneous to pool. Don't force a pooled estimate across incomparable designs. Second pitfall: forgetting grey literature and trial registries, which inflates publication bias.

**Tools:** PRISMA 2020, PROSPERO, Covidence/Rayyan, RoB 2, ROBINS-I, AMSTAR 2
