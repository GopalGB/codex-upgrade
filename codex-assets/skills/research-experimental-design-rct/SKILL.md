# research-experimental-design-rct

Causation needs three things: covariation, temporal precedence, and ruling out alternatives — randomization is what rules out alternatives by balancing confounders (known AND unknown) across arms in expectation. **Randomize properly**: computer-generated allocation, concealed from enrollers; use **block randomization** to keep arm sizes even and **stratified randomization** to balance key prognostic variables.

Include a real control (placebo or active comparator, not just "nothing"). **Blind** wherever possible: single-blind (participants), double-blind (participants + assessors) to kill expectancy and assessor bias. Choose **between-subjects** (each person one condition — no carryover, needs more n) vs **within-subjects** (each person all conditions — more powerful, but counterbalance order to defeat practice/fatigue). Use a **factorial design** to test multiple factors and their interactions efficiently.

Analyze by **intention-to-treat** (analyze as randomized, regardless of compliance) — per-protocol analysis reintroduces selection bias. Report per **CONSORT** (flow diagram + checklist) and preregister.

Pitfall: breaking randomization by excluding dropouts non-randomly. Second pitfall: too-small n -> underpowered null that's uninterpretable; size it via power analysis first.

**Tools:** Randomization (block/stratified), control group, single/double blind, factorial design, CONSORT, ITT analysis
