# career-offer-evaluation

An offer is more than the number. Build a **weighted decision matrix**: list factors (total comp, growth/learning, manager & team quality, role scope, company trajectory/stability, WLB, location/remote, mission), weight each by *your* priorities, score each offer 1–5, and compute weighted totals — then sanity-check against your gut. **Normalize total comp** across offers: base + realistic equity value (for startups, discount heavily — most equity goes to zero; ask preferred valuation, dilution, vesting/cliff, strike) + bonus + sign-on, adjusted for cost of living.

Weight non-comp factors heavily early-career: **manager and team quality** and **rate of learning** compound into future comp far more than a marginal base difference. Assess risk: startup stage/runway/funding vs big-co stability; team you'd join, not just the brand. Use the interview to gather this (ask about on-call, growth, why the role is open, recent attrition).

**What top candidates do differently:** they optimize for slope (growth trajectory) over the y-intercept (starting comp), do reference-checks on the manager (backchannel), and treat unvested equity at a risky startup as ~$0 in the math.

**Common mistake:** chasing the highest headline number while ignoring a bad manager, no growth path, or worthless equity; over-valuing prestige; and deciding emotionally without writing down the factors. The highest-comp offer is frequently not the best career move.

**Tools:** total-comp normalization, weighted decision matrix, equity valuation, growth/manager/team signals, risk assessment
