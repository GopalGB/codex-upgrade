# career-interview-debrief-iteration

The search is a feedback loop; the candidates who improve fastest **debrief every interview within 24h while memory is fresh.** Log: questions asked, what went well, what stalled, the exact problem you couldn't solve, and your energy/communication. Convert each gap into a concrete drill (re-solve the stumped problem at 1d/7d spacing; rewrite the behavioral story that rambled).

Diagnose by **funnel stage**: many applies but no screens → top-of-funnel (resume keywords, channel, referrals — A/B test two resume versions). Screens but no onsites → tech-screen prep (patterns, communication). Onsites but no offers → behavioral/system-design/closing/leveling — the subtle stuff. Different stage = different fix; don't just 'try harder.' Politely **request feedback** after a rejection ('any feedback to help me improve?') — some recruiters give gold.

**What top candidates do differently:** they treat early interviews as paid practice for top-choice companies (sequence safe→reach), keep a running 'mistakes I repeat' list, and mock-interview to fix communication, not just correctness.

**Common mistake:** spiraling on rejection without analyzing it, repeating the same mistake across loops (no debrief = no learning), changing everything at once so you can't tell what worked, and never asking for feedback. Rejections are the highest-signal data in the search — mine them.

**Tools:** post-interview log, funnel-stage diagnosis, rejection-feedback request, spaced re-practice, A/B resume testing
