# retail-planogram-space

A planogram maps every SKU to a shelf position and **facings** count. Size facings so a SKU holds enough to cover the replenishment cycle: **facings = (daily units x days-of-supply) / units-per-facing-depth**, preventing mid-cycle out-of-stocks on fast movers.

Use the **space-to-sales index** (SKU's % of space / its % of category sales): index ~1.0 is balanced; >1.2 over-spaced (shrink), <0.8 under-spaced on a winner (expand). The expert move: place high-margin/impulse and proven sellers at **eye level (the 'buy level', ~4-5 ft)** and at the start of the aisle in the dominant traffic-flow direction; relegate low-margin bulky staples to bottom shelves (shoppers stoop for needs, not wants). Block by **brand/segment** vertically for shop-ability, and put complements adjacent (pasta next to sauce) to lift basket.

Pitfalls: equal facings for unequal sellers (out-of-stock on hits, overstock on dogs), ignoring **case-pack rounding** (facings must hold at least one case to avoid daily restock labor), and vendor-driven planograms that grab share-of-shelf beyond share-of-sales. Re-flow after every assortment change and validate against actual movement, not the vendor's deck.

**Tools:** space-to-sales index, days-of-supply facings, eye-level zoning, JDA/Blue Yonder space
