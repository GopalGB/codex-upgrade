# ba-process-mapping

Map the AS-IS before designing the TO-BE. Choose the notation by purpose: swimlane (cross-functional flowchart) when handoffs between roles are the issue — lanes = actors, boxes = activities, diamonds = decisions, arrows = flow; BPMN when you need rigor/automation (events, gateways, pools); value-stream map (VSM) when you're attacking time and waste — it overlays cycle time, wait time, and inventory between steps. Walk the actual process with the people who do it (gemba), not the documented ideal. Capture per step: actor, system, cycle time (work time), wait/queue time, and %Complete-and-Accurate (fraction passing downstream without rework). Compute lead time (total elapsed, including waits) vs process/cycle time (hands-on) — the gap is the waste; process efficiency = cycle/lead is often shockingly low (single digits). Expert moves: flag handoffs (where errors and delay concentrate), rework loops (rejection paths back upstream), and decision points with unclear rules; quantify the bottleneck by the longest queue, not the slowest task. Hunt the 8 wastes (DOWNTIME) — esp. waiting, handoffs, and defects/rework. Pitfalls: mapping the official SOP instead of reality; too much detail too early (start at the value-stream altitude, then drill the bottleneck); no time/quality data so improvements are guesses; omitting the exception/unhappy paths where most pain lives; redesigning before measuring the baseline.

**Tools:** BPMN, swimlane diagrams, value-stream mapping, Visio, Lucidchart
