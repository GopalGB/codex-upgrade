# retail-category-management

Category management treats each category as a strategic business unit. The classic **8-step process**: 1) Define the category (consumer decision tree), 2) Assign a **role**, 3) Assess (scorecard), 4) Set performance targets, 5) Strategies, 6) Tactics, 7) Implement, 8) Review.

**Roles** drive everything: **Destination** (be the store of choice — deep assortment, sharp price, e.g. diapers at a baby store), **Routine/Preferred** (consistent value, the bulk), **Occasional/Seasonal**, **Convenience** (round out the trip, take margin). Tactics flow from role: destination = price-aggressive + broad; convenience = margin-aggressive + edited.

The expert move: align assortment, price, promo, and shelf to the role — don't price a convenience category like a destination (you leave margin) or under-stock a destination (you lose the trip). Use a **category scorecard** (sales, share, margin, GMROI, turns, units/transaction) vs market.

Pitfalls: letting the **category captain** (lead vendor) optimize for their own brands — validate their planogram against neutral data. Defining categories by supplier logistics instead of the **consumer decision tree** (how the shopper actually navigates the need). Review quarterly; roles shift with the market.

**Tools:** category roles, category scorecard, 8-step ECR process, role-based tactics
