# prod-single-tasking

Multitasking is a myth for cognitive work-the brain doesn't parallelize, it rapidly **task-switches**, and each switch incurs a cost: studies show switching can cut effective productivity by up to ~40% and raise error rates, while **attention residue** (Leroy) means part of your mind stays stuck on the prior task for minutes after each switch. Single-tasking is the fix: work on exactly one thing until a natural stopping point. Operationalize it-(1) one task visible at a time (close other apps/tabs, full-screen the work); (2) a written 'next task' note so you don't context-switch to 'remember' what's next; (3) pair with Pomodoro or a time block so the single-task commitment has a defined duration; (4) batch the urge to switch by capturing intrusive thoughts on a pad rather than acting on them. The 'one thing' lens (Keller): ask 'what's the ONE thing I can do such that everything else is easier or unnecessary?' and do only that. The failure mode is performative busyness-feeling productive while juggling, when juggling is precisely what makes each item slower and buggier. Depth on one beats shallow on five.

**Tools:** single-tasking, monotasking, switch cost, attention residue, one-thing focus
