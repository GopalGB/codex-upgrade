# research-validity-reliability

Separate the two: **reliability** = consistency (would you get the same result again); **validity** = accuracy (are you measuring what you claim). You can be reliable but invalid (a consistently biased scale), but you can't be valid without being reliable.

Reliability types and their stats: **internal consistency** (Cronbach's alpha >= .70, or McDonald's omega which is often better), **test-retest** (correlation across two timepoints), **inter-rater** (Cohen's kappa for 2 raters, Fleiss for 3+, ICC for continuous). Validity types: **content** (items cover the construct), **construct** (convergent + discriminant — correlates with what it should, not with what it shouldn't), **criterion** (predicts an external standard), and at the study level: **internal validity** (causal claim is sound — threats: history, maturation, selection, regression to the mean, testing, instrumentation), **external validity** (generalizes), **statistical-conclusion validity** (right test, adequate power).

For qualitative work, use **Lincoln & Guba's trustworthiness**: credibility (member checking, triangulation), transferability (thick description), dependability (audit trail), confirmability (reflexivity).

Pitfall: claiming high reliability (alpha) as if it proved validity — it doesn't. Second pitfall: ignoring internal-validity threats in quasi-experiments, then asserting causation.

**Tools:** internal/external/construct validity, Cronbach's alpha, test-retest, Cohen's/Fleiss kappa, Lincoln & Guba trustworthiness, threats checklist
