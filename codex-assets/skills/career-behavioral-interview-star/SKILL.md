# career-behavioral-interview-star

Behavioral rounds test ownership, collaboration, conflict, ambiguity, and impact via past behavior. Answer every 'tell me about a time' with **STAR: Situation (brief context), Task (your specific responsibility), Action (what *you* did — the bulk, in detail), Result (quantified outcome + a Learning).** Keep S+T to ~20%, A to ~60%, R to ~20%; total 2–3 minutes.

Build a **story bank of 6–8 reusable stories** mapped to common themes: biggest accomplishment, conflict with a coworker, missed deadline/failure, ambiguous problem, persuaded without authority, mistake you owned, tight deadline, disagreed-and-committed. One strong story often covers 3–4 questions. For Amazon-style loops, map stories to the company's leadership principles explicitly.

**What top candidates do differently:** they say **'I' not 'we'** (interviewers need *your* contribution), pick stories with real stakes and a real result, show self-awareness in failure stories (what you learned and changed), and quantify outcomes even for 'soft' stories.

**Common mistake:** rambling with no structure (S/T eats all the time, Action gets cut), hiding behind 'we' so your role is invisible, choosing a fake-failure ('I work too hard'), and having no metric in the Result. Practice out loud and time it — unstructured answers feel disorganized even when the underlying work was great.

**Tools:** STAR(L) structure, story bank, leadership-principle mapping, I-not-we framing, metric-led result
