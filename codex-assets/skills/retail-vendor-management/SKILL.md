# retail-vendor-management

Treat vendors as a portfolio with a **scorecard**: OTIF delivery %, fill rate, quality/defect rate, lead-time reliability, pricing competitiveness, and innovation. Rank and tier — concentrate volume with top performers for leverage, dual-source critical categories to avoid single-point failure.

Negotiation levers beyond unit cost: **payment terms** (net-30 vs net-60 = free working capital; each extra 30 days is real cash), **MOQ** (lower = less inventory risk on new items), **trade allowances** (co-op advertising, **markdown money/MDA** to share clearance risk, slotting/new-item fees), volume rebates, and **return-to-vendor (RTV)** rights on defects/overstock. The expert move: negotiate **markdown money up front** so the vendor co-funds the clearance of slow movers — it protects your maintained margin and aligns incentives on the buy.

Pitfalls: chasing lowest unit cost while ignoring terms/reliability (a cheap unreliable vendor costs more in safety stock and lost sales), no documented compliance program so late/short shipments go unpenalized (use **chargebacks** for OTIF/labeling/routing violations), and over-concentration creating dependency. Review scorecards quarterly; tie next season's volume allocation to performance.

**Tools:** vendor scorecard, OTIF compliance, MOQ, payment terms, markdown money/co-op, chargebacks
