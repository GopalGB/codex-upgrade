# prod-energy-management

Manage energy, not just time (Loehr/Schwartz)-you have ~4 capacities: physical, emotional, mental, spiritual, and performance comes from oscillating between expenditure and renewal, not constant output. The mechanism is the **ultradian rhythm**: the brain cycles through ~90-120 min of rising-then-falling focus, after which it needs a 15-20 min recovery; pushing past the trough produces diminishing, error-prone work. Structure the day in 90-minute work pulses separated by real renewal (walk, snack, eyes off screens-not email). Map your **chronotype** (Pink's *When*): most people get a morning peak (analytic/deep work), an early-afternoon trough (do admin/email/low-stakes), and a late-afternoon recovery (creative/exploratory). Match task to phase: deep work in the peak, shallow work in the trough-never burn your one peak block on email. The failure mode is treating all hours as equal and scheduling your hardest task whenever there's a free slot. Protect sleep (the master input), and take breaks *before* exhaustion, not after-recovery is a strategy, not a reward.

**Tools:** ultradian rhythm, chronotype, 90-minute cycles, energy quadrants, strategic renewal
