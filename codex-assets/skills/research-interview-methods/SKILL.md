# research-interview-methods

Pick the format by how much you already know: **structured** for comparability across many respondents, **semi-structured** (the workhorse) for a guided-but-flexible conversation, **unstructured** for exploration. Build a semi-structured **guide** as a funnel: broad grand-tour question first ("Walk me through a typical day..."), then narrowing, with planned **probes** ("Can you give an example?", "What did that feel like?") under each.

Ask open, non-leading questions — "How did the rollout go?" not "The rollout was frustrating, right?". Use silence and laddering ("why does that matter to you?") to reach depth. Avoid double-barreled and jargon questions. Record (with consent) and transcribe via Whisper/otter.ai, then verify the transcript against audio for analysis-critical passages.

Gauge **data saturation** — stop recruiting when new interviews yield no new codes (often ~12-20 for homogeneous samples, more for diverse ones). Optionally do **member checking** (return findings to participants) for credibility.

Pitfall: the interviewer talking too much / steering answers — aim for the participant doing 80%+ of the talking. Second pitfall: leading probes that plant the answer you expected.

**Tools:** Semi-structured guide, funnel technique, laddering probes, member checking, otter.ai/Whisper transcription, saturation
