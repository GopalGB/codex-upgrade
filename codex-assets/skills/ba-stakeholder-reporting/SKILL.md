# ba-stakeholder-reporting

Lead with the answer — BLUF (bottom line up front). The first sentence states the conclusion and the ask ('Recommend we sunset Plan B; it's −18% margin and 4% of revenue — approval needed by Friday'), not the methodology. Structure with the Pyramid Principle (Minto): governing recommendation at the top, supported by 3-4 grouped arguments (MECE — mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive), each backed by data; the reader can stop at any level and still have a complete answer. Frame the setup with SCQA: Situation (shared context) → Complication (what changed/the problem) → Question (the decision) → Answer (your recommendation). Tier the artifact: one-page exec summary (decision + 3 reasons + ask), appendix with the analysis, raw data on request — executives read top-down, never make them dig. Expert moves: quantify the so-what ($ and %, vs target/last period); state the decision you need and the deadline; give a recommendation with the tradeoff, not options without a pick; pre-empt the top objection. Pitfalls: chronological narration of what you did instead of what you found; charts with no takeaway title (title every chart with the insight: 'Q3 churn rose to 6%, driven by SMB', not 'Monthly Churn'); hedging that hides the call; passive voice; >1 page that nobody reads. Know whether the reader wants to inform, decide, or approve — write to that.

**Tools:** Pyramid Principle, BLUF, SCQA, exec summary
