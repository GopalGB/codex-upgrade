# career-recruiter-cold-outreach

Cold outreach converts when it's **short, personalized, and has one specific ask.** Three targets: (1) recruiters (fast inbound), (2) hiring managers (highest signal), (3) potential referrers at the company. Structure (4–6 sentences): a personalized hook (their work/post/the team) → one line on why you fit (a metric or directly relevant project) → a *specific* ask → make it effortless to say yes. For a referral: 'I'm applying to <role> at <co>; I built <relevant thing>. Would you be open to referring me? Happy to send a blurb + resume to make it easy.'

Channel: LinkedIn connect-with-note or email (find via Hunter/RocketReach pattern). Keep it skimmable on mobile. **Follow up once** after ~5–7 business days; then stop. Volume + personalization both matter — a templated skeleton with 2 genuinely customized lines scales.

**What top candidates do differently:** they ask for a referral, not a job ('would you refer me' is a small, concrete yes), give the referrer a ready-made blurb + tailored resume, and target 2nd-degree connections and recent posters (active = responsive). They lead with value/specificity, never 'I'm looking for opportunities.'

**Common mistake:** generic copy-paste ('I came across your profile and would love to connect'), asking for too much from a stranger ('can we hop on a 30-min call'), a vague ask, walls of text, and never following up — or following up five times. One precise, easy-to-grant ask beats a hundred generic blasts.

**Tools:** personalized hook, specific ask, brevity, referral request scripts, follow-up cadence
